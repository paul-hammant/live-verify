8 billion smartphones already have OCR built-in. The verification layer is the missing piece that unlocks instant trust for physical documents—without blockchain, without apps, without QR codes.

University degrees
Insurance certificates

Inspection reports

From immigration documents at borders to supply chain certifications at ports—466 documented use cases spanning every major industry.

Why Now?

Apple and Google will inevitably integrate this. They already have the OCR infrastructure. Adding verification prevents fraud, protects users, and positions them as gatekeepers of digital trust.

The question isn't if they'll integrate OCR-to-hash verification—it's when. And who builds the infrastructure they'll verify against.

The Window

Before Apple and Google build their own verification endpoints, there's an opportunity to become the authoritative verification layer—the infrastructure that government agencies, universities, hospitals, and enterprises use to host their document hashes.

First mover advantage means domain authority, network effects, and becoming the default standard before the platforms commoditize verification.

Technical Foundation

This isn't vaporware. The proof-of-concept demonstrates the complete flow:

✓ Client-side OCR (privacy-preserving)
✓ SHA-256 hashing with normalization
✓ Domain-binding for issuer verification
✓ Works with existing infrastructure

The hard work is done. What's needed now is enterprise deployment, security hardening, and market penetration before platform integration forces commoditization.

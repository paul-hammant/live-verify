FUTURE VISION

A Plausible Next Step for Mobile Trust

If Apple and Google integrate OCR-to-hash verification into their camera apps, recognizing a verify: line in the viewfinder could automatically check the claim and show:

✅VERIFIED
❌FAILS VERIFICATION

Note: Imagine status.uscis.gov or verify.carfax.com as domains hosting verifications instead of paul-hammant.github.io (static/free demo of tech)

Why this could become a camera feature

Billions of smartphones already ship OCR. A privacy-preserving “Verified / Denied” overlay is a plausible next step: it reduces fraud while keeping the verification flow inside the camera app. It’s not guaranteed—but it fits the direction platforms already took with Live Text and on-device ML.

Note: scanning from screens can introduce moiré patterns; higher-capability OCR (as used by platform camera apps) can improve reliability.

Market Impact

A large slice of everyday verification can become faster and more private: receipts, licenses, permits, certificates, and credentials checked on the spot, without phone calls or portal uploads. The issuer-side infrastructure can be as simple as hash lookups; the commercial opportunity is in integrations, governance, and operations.

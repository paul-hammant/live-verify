# Mandatory Workplace Postings

Federal and state labor law posters required to be displayed in workplaces. Typically posted in break rooms, near time clocks, or in common areas where employees gather.

## Included Posting Types

- [Federal Minimum Wage Posters](view.html?doc=federal-minimum-wage-labor-posters) - FLSA requirements
- [State Labor/Wage Posters](view.html?doc=state-labor-wage-posters) - State-specific requirements
- [OSHA Safety Posters](view.html?doc=osha-workplace-safety-posters) - Workplace safety rights
- [EEO/EEOC Posters](view.html?doc=eeo-eeoc-posters) - Anti-discrimination notices
- [FMLA Posters](view.html?doc=fmla-posters) - Family and medical leave
- [Workers' Comp Postings](view.html?doc=workers-comp-insurance-posting) - Insurance information
- [NLRA/Unionization Posters](view.html?doc=nlra-unionization-poster) - Organizing rights
- [Unemployment Insurance Postings](view.html?doc=unemployment-insurance-posting) - Benefits information
- [Emergency Evacuation Maps](view.html?doc=emergency-evacuation-maps) - Exit routes
- [Smoking Prohibition Signs](view.html?doc=smoking-prohibition-signs) - No-smoking policies

## Common Display Requirements

- Posted in conspicuous location
- Accessible to all employees
- Must be current year's version (updated annually)
- Multiple languages may be required
- Penalties for non-compliance ($100-$50,000+ per violation)

## Verification Value

**Problem:** Outdated or fake workplace postings:
- Old posters with incorrect minimum wage
- Fake posters omitting employee rights
- Missing required notices to avoid compliance
- Altered posters removing union information

**Solution:** Employees can verify workplace postings are genuine and current. Labor inspectors can quickly validate compliance during site visits.

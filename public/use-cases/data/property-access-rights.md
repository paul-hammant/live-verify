## What is Property Access Rights Verification?

When someone claims the right to enter a property — to a locksmith, a delivery person, or a neighbor — how do they prove it? Currently: ID + utility bill, or "trust me."

Property access rights verification provides **on-demand proof** that a person has a legitimate right to access a property, whether as owner, tenant, lodger, or authorized guest.

**The locksmith scenario:**
1. Person calls: "I'm locked out of my house"
2. Locksmith arrives, asks: "Can you prove you have a right to this property?"
3. Person presents verifiable document (not just a PDF)
4. Locksmith scans → verification confirms → opens lock

## Types of Access Rights

| Right Type | Document | Who stores hash | Duration |
|------------|----------|-----------------|----------|
| **Owner** | Land Registry title / County deed | Government registry | Permanent until transfer |
| **Tenant** | Tenancy agreement | Landlord or letting agent | Term of tenancy |
| **Lodger** | Lodger agreement | Homeowner | Term of agreement |
| **Short-term guest** | Airbnb/booking confirmation | Platform or property owner | Duration of stay |
| **House-sitter** | House-sitting authorization | Property owner | Specified dates |
| **Keyholder** | Keyholder authorization | Property owner | Until revoked |

**The keyholder problem:**
- Owner on holiday, keyholder needs locksmith (lost key)
- Keyholder is not owner, not tenant
- How does locksmith verify keyholder has legitimate access?

**Solution:** Owner creates keyholder authorization (stored on owner's domain or third-party keyholder service), keyholder presents when needed.

## The Locksmith Workflow

```
PERSON CALLS LOCKSMITH
"I'm locked out of my property"
            ↓
LOCKSMITH ARRIVES
"Can you prove you have a right to this property?"
            ↓
PERSON PRESENTS VERIFICATION
    ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Owner?     → Land Registry title copy       │
    │ Tenant?    → Verified tenancy agreement     │
    │ Guest?     → Booking confirmation           │
    │ Keyholder? → Keyholder authorization        │
    └─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
            ↓
LOCKSMITH SCANS
            ↓
    ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ ✓ VERIFIED                                  │
    │ Property: 14 Maple Avenue                   │
    │ Right holder: James Smith                   │
    │ Type: Owner (Land Registry confirmed)       │
    │                                             │
    │ + Check ID matches name                     │
    └─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
            ↓
LOCKSMITH OPENS LOCK
(Creates work order referencing verification)
```

## Who Stores the Hash?

| Document Type | Hash stored by | Why trustworthy |
|---------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Land Registry title** | gov.uk/land-registry | Government registry, cross-referenced with actual ownership |
| **County deed (US)** | County recorder | Government office, public record |
| **Tenancy agreement** | Landlord or letting agent domain | Professional letting agents are regulated; landlords have professional liability |
| **Booking confirmation** | Property owner or platform | Owner's domain or platform (Airbnb, Booking.com) |
| **Keyholder authorization** | Property owner or keyholder service | Owner takes responsibility; third-party services add accountability |

## Third-Party Use

**Locksmiths** — Verify right to access before opening
**Delivery services** — Verify recipient is legitimate occupant (high-value deliveries)
**Neighbors** — Challenge suspicious access attempts with verifiable evidence
**Police** — On-scene verification of claimed access rights
**Property managers** — Verify sublet/guest arrangements are authorized
**Insurance** — Evidence that access was by authorized person (after incidents)

## Data Verified

**Property address**, **right holder name**, **type of right** (owner/tenant/guest/keyholder), **validity period**, **any restrictions**, **issuing authority** (Land Registry, landlord, owner).

## Data Visible After Verification

**Status Indications:**
- **Active** — Right currently valid
- **Expired** — Past end date (tenancy ended, booking over)
- **Revoked** — Authorization withdrawn by owner/landlord
- **Superseded** — Replaced by new agreement
- **Pending** — Not yet active (future booking, tenancy not yet started)

## Verification Architecture

**The Problem:**
- Current proof of address: utility bill + ID (easily faked, often outdated)
- Locksmiths rely on trust and judgment
- Criminals exploit this to access properties they have no right to
- No standard way for tenants to prove tenancy on-demand

**The Fix:** Each type of access right has a verifiable document issued by the appropriate authority:
- Owners: Land Registry (government)
- Tenants: Landlord/agent (regulated profession)
- Guests: Property owner or booking platform
- Keyholders: Property owner or keyholder service

**On-demand, not posted:** Unlike estate clearance (mandatory site notice), property access rights are presented when challenged — carried by the person, shown on request.
